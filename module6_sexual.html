Understanding Sexually Transmitted Infections

Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are infections passed from one person to another through sexual contact. Common examples include chlamydia, gonorrhea, and HIV.

  • Always use protection during intercourse
  • Get tested regularly
  • Practice open and honest communication

Contraception

Contraceptives help prevent pregnancy and some STIs. There are many types:

  • Barrier methods (e.g., condoms)
  • Hormonal methods (e.g., pills, IUDs)
  • Emergency contraception

Consent & Communication

Consent means giving permission freely, enthusiastically, and clearly. It's essential in all relationships.

  • Always ask and respect the answer
  • No means no. Silence is not consent.
  • Consent can be withdrawn at any time
