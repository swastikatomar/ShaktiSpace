Sexual Health FAQ

Get informed and empowered with these answers.

What does STI stand for?

STI stands for Sexually Transmitted Infection. It refers to infections passed from one person to another through sexual contact. Examples include chlamydia, gonorrhea, and HIV.

What are the different types of contraception?

There are several types of contraception including barrier methods (like condoms), hormonal methods (like birth control pills), and emergency contraception. Each method has its own effectiveness, and it's important to choose the one that works best for you.

What is consent?

Consent means giving permission freely, enthusiastically, and clearly. It is a mutual agreement and can be withdrawn at any time during a sexual encounter. Always ask and respect the answer.

How can I protect myself from STIs?

The most effective way to protect yourself from STIs is through abstinence or using protection, such as condoms. Regular STI testing and open communication with your partner are also essential to sexual health.

Are there vaccines for STIs?

Yes, there are vaccines available for certain STIs. The HPV (Human Papillomavirus) vaccine can help prevent cervical cancer and other cancers caused by HPV. The Hepatitis B vaccine is also available and can prevent Hepatitis B infection.

What is sexual health?

Sexual health is a state of well-being in which sexual rights are respected, individuals can have safe sexual experiences, and individuals are free from any sexual disorders or diseases. It includes physical, emotional, and social aspects of sexuality.

How do I know if I need to get tested for STIs?

If you are sexually active, especially with multiple partners or without using protection, it's important to get tested regularly. If you notice any unusual symptoms, such as sores, rashes, or discharge, you should consult a healthcare provider immediately.

What should I do if I have a question about my sexual health?

If you have any concerns about your sexual health, it's important to talk to a trusted healthcare provider. They can answer questions, provide advice, and help you understand your options.

What are some signs of unhealthy sexual behavior?

Signs of unhealthy sexual behavior include coercion, manipulation, and lack of consent. A healthy sexual relationship is based on mutual respect, trust, and communication. If you feel uncomfortable or unsafe, it's important to seek help.

Where can I get more information about sexual health?

For more information on sexual health, you can refer to trusted websites such as Planned Parenthood and WHO Sexual Health.
